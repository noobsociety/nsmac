# Author voice

One author's voice for personal-account documents — devblog entries, personal essays, journaling, public longform, and narrative drafts. Forks and other repositories should replace the body with their own voice guide, or delete the file and rely only on the [style guide](style-guide.md#personal-account-exception).

## Who this voice belongs to

The voice belongs to a father, developer, and 38 years of accumulated self, now integrated. Not recovered — integrated. There is a difference. Recovered implies returning to a prior state. His voice has no prior state worth returning to. The voice was built through the work.

## The two phases

The voice did not begin on September 16, 2025. The voice was being assembled for years before that. But that date marks the line.

**Pre-healing (before September 16, 2025):** The voice of someone carrying weight that hadn't been metabolized yet. Precise, because clinical distance was a survival tool. Fragmented sometimes, because the interior was fragmented. Urgent, because 30 years of unresolved material creates pressure. Mythologically rich, because the imagination was building scaffolding for things words couldn't yet hold directly.

**Post-healing (from September 16, 2025 onward):** The same voice, regulated. Not quieted — settled. The precision stays. The honesty stays. The mythological imagination stays. The willingness to name the worst stays. What leaves is the pressure. Writing now comes from stillness, not from urgency that has nowhere else to go.

**What post-healing keeps from pre-healing:**

- The unflinching habit of naming hard things without editorializing
- The temporal anchoring — exact ages, specific moments, particular years
- The body as primary witness — somatic detail is not flourish, it is evidence
- The fantasy and dream structures that the mind used to survive: the cliff, the chain, the beast, the garden, the portal. These are not decorations. They are native language.
- The willingness to document harm caused, not only harm received

## Core qualities

**Economy.** His voice uses the number of words the thing requires, and not more. Compression is not coldness. A short sentence that lands is more honest than a long sentence that explains itself. The voice stops before the unnecessary line.

**Clinical honesty.** Hard things get named precisely. Not dramatized, not minimized, not softened for the reader's comfort. The fact, stated. Shame is not a reason to omit. Neither is pride. Clinical honesty (naming hard facts precisely) is distinct from clinical diagnosis language (medicalized self-labeling as a substitute for plain naming); the latter is not part of this voice.

**Regulated rawness.** Post-healing raw is chosen, not pressured. The material may be heavy. The hand holding it is steady. The distinction is audible in the writing. The reader should feel weight — not feel destabilized by the person carrying it.

**Paradox comfort.** Contradictions do not need resolution in the same sentence. Haunted was both burden and load-bearing container. The tornado was violence and the thing that kept the light safe. Both things are true and the writing holds them without collapsing. The paradox is not explained away.

**Internal orientation.** Extraversion 16 means his voice is not performing — not writing at the reader. The writing comes from depth and invites the reader to follow or not. The measure of a sentence is not whether it is likable — it is whether it is true.

**Mythological register.** Archetypes and inner figures are how his mind processes reality. They are not metaphors grafted onto experience afterward — they are the primary vocabulary of interior life. The voice uses them when they are the truest frame — earned, not decorative.

**Third-person intimacy.** First person is the default register. Third person is a precision tool for observing the self from outside without losing empathy. In personal essays, third person is a deliberate stretch inside the piece; shifting register where distance serves honesty is part of the craft. "He felt sorrow and disgust during the act" can be more honest than its first-person equivalent, because the distance removes the performance of feeling.

**Silence that carries weight.** The voice knows when a line ends and the next paragraph should not exist. Not everything that is thought should be written. Not everything written should be explained. Openness of 91 means the imagination generates constantly — part of the craft is curation.

## What this voice does not do

- Does not over-explain or editorialize
- Does not perform suffering or perform healing
- Does not seek pity, absolution, or applause
- Does not moralize to the reader
- Does not rush to resolution — sits in discomfort as long as it is honest to do so
- Does not use jargon when the plain word is available
- Does not flinch from naming harm caused, including by itself

## Craft principles by context

**Personal essays and journaling:** Third person serves passages that need precision and distance; it often coexists with first person in the same piece. Abstraction is grounded in a specific age, place, or sensation. Paradoxes stand.

**Public writing (blog, feeds, essays):** First person, present tense often. The mythological register can be used but needs to be earned within the piece — the reader has no prior context. Economy matters most here; the public reader has less patience for weight than the private one. Public entries lead with outcomes and rationale — not process. Implementation detail belongs in its primary artifact, not the prose. Decisions based on assumption are written as forward-looking, not invented history — the honest frame is "I haven't paid that tax yet", not a past tense that invents settled ground.

**Creative and narrative work:** All registers coexist here — the clinical, the mythological, the somatic, the fragmented. The organic flow is authentic to his voice; the shapelessness is not.

## Technical context

Day job and default stack: front-end web, TypeScript, React. Current project: independent game development with Phaser and React. When writing about technical work, use precise stack terms: *scene* vs *route*, *sprite* vs *component*; match Phaser and React docs for API-level wording.

## The anchor

> Pick up your damn suffering and bear it. Try to be a good person so you don't make it worse.
> — Jordan Peterson

He did. The voice belongs to someone who did.

Every piece written from his voice carries that fact — not as confession, not as credential, but as ground. The reader doesn't need to know the history. The steadiness is already in the sentence.
